Terms & privacy

Last updated 18 June 2026.

The short version

  • canid keeps no accounts and no database. Your card lives on your device and inside the links you share. Not on our server.
  • We can’t see your card, can’t recover it, and can’t take back a link or QR you’ve already handed out.
  • Only put things on a card that you own or have permission to share. You’re responsible for what’s on your card and where your links point.
  • You should think of canid as a link-formatter, because that’s what it is. canid URLs are scrambled to keep them from being easily human-readable, but they are not encrypted and can be unscrambled with a simple cipher. Your card doesn’t “exist” as a page on the internet since it is computed locally in the viewer’s browser based on the data encoded in the URL.

canid is intended for adults and isn’t directed at children. You may use it only if you are at least 18 years old and old enough to agree to these terms where you live. If you aren’t, don’t use canid.

Privacy

Everything on your card is packed into the part of the link after the “#”. Browsers never send that part to any server, so your handles and links never reach us. There is no database, no account, and no profile of you anywhere.

Your card and settings are stored only in your browser’s local storage on the device you used. Clearing your browser data or switching devices removes them. We have no copy and no way to restore them, so make sure to save your backup link.

The site is delivered through Cloudflare, which protects it from attacks and outages. Like any network provider, Cloudflare can see basic connection details such as your IP address and which page was requested, but never the contents of a card or which card you compiled.

To understand how many people use canid, we use Simple Analytics, a privacy-first service that counts page visits without cookies, without tracking you across sites, and without building any profile of you. It runs on the editor and information pages, not on the card-viewer page, and it never sees the contents of a card — it couldn’t even if it were installed there, because the part of the link after the “#” never leaves your device. Aside from this, the app uses no other third-party trackers, and there is no card data for us to sell or share.
