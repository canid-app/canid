How it works

  1. Add the handles or links you want to share.
  2. Sort them into groups for different audiences or occasions.
  3. Show your QR code in person, print it on a flyer, or drop your link. Whoever opens it sees only that group, and can’t tell the others exist.

Your data

canid has no database, no accounts, and no backend. Your handles are packed into a single long URL, and that URL is your card. It never touches a server: when someone opens your link, their own browser builds the card on their screen.

Since no data lives online, this isn’t for everyone. Any link you’ve shared keeps working forever but your editable card lives only on your device, so clearing your browser or switching phones means rebuilding it from scratch unless you kept a backup or a link. And because each link is a fixed snapshot, you can’t edit or revoke one you’ve handed out. Treat it like a paper business card: once it’s out, that version is out. You also can’t know who, or how many people, opened your card. If you’re trying to monetize an audience or track engagement, canid probably isn’t for you.

In return, you can keep as many separate profiles as you like, each with its own handles, groups, and color for a different part of your life. All with no password to remember, no ads, and no data sold.

For the night (or day) out

canid was designed for sharing in dark, loud, or busy environments like clubs, parties, markets, and meetups. That means big tap targets, a native dark mode that won’t flash-bang anyone, and a quick-access QR code. But, it works just as well online if you’re okay with long links.

Make it yours

canid is minimal and fast, but you can still give your card a personal touch. Add your name (or don’t) and choose from millions of colors and four typefaces. Cards can hold your socials, phone, email, and location, and anyone who opens one can save it straight to their contacts.

Install the app

canid runs in any browser. It’s highly recommended to install canid on your phone to keep your profile saved on your device and easily bring up the QR code.

iPhone & iPad
Safari

  1. Open this page in Safari (not Chrome or Firefox).
  2. Tap the Share button. It’s the box with an arrow pointing up, at the bottom of the screen.
  3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Tap Add in the top-right corner.

Android
Chrome

  1. Open this page in Chrome.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (⋮) in the top-right corner.
  3. Tap Add to Home screen or Install app.
  4. Tap Install in the prompt.

You can also write your card to a blank NFC tag: open the editor, tap the NFC icon in its top bar, then hold your phone to the tag.

Android
Samsung Internet

  1. Open this page in Samsung Internet.
  2. Tap the menu icon (three lines) at the bottom-right.
  3. Tap Add page to, then Home screen.
  4. Tap Add to confirm.

How it works & FAQ

A few things to know first

canid works differently from most apps. Before you make a card:

  • Your card isn’t a webpage. It’s packed into the link itself and rebuilt in the viewer’s browser. Nothing is stored on a server.
  • A shared link is permanent. Once you hand out a link or QR, you can’t edit or take it back, just like a paper card or sending someone a handle name.
  • Your editing copy lives only on this device. A backup restores everything; a saved card link can rebuild that one card. Without either, clearing your browser or losing your phone means starting over.
  • Scrambled isn’t encrypted. Anyone you give a link or QR to can read everything in it, so only add what you’re okay sharing.
